# Generate Brand Names

## Goal

Generate short, distinctive names that feel premium, timeless, and brandable rather than descriptive. Avoid generic AI/startup buzzwords and favor names with credible roots in technical, historical, natural, mythological, linguistic, or scientific domains.

## Workflow

1. Identify the product category, audience, emotional tone, and any hard constraints from the user prompt.
2. If critical context is missing, make a minimal assumption and state it briefly before the names. Do not block unless the product itself is unclear.
3. Generate names from varied source domains:
   - Geology, minerals, metallurgy, and materials
   - Astronomy, orbital mechanics, navigation, and cartography
   - Physics, optics, mathematics, and systems theory
   - Latin, Greek, Sanskrit, Japanese, Old Norse, and other historical languages
   - Architecture, ancient infrastructure, instruments, and craft traditions
   - Mythology and historical figures only when the reference is subtle and not overused
4. Filter out names that sound generic, trend-chasing, hard to say, hard to spell, or too close to existing famous brands.
5. Present the strongest names first. Prioritize fewer excellent names over many weak names.

## Naming Standards

Prefer names that are:

- 4-10 characters when possible, usually one or two syllables.
- Easy to pronounce aloud after one glance.
- Visually clean in a logo, URL, and app header.
- Abstract enough to support future product expansion.
- Distinctive enough to be searchable.
- Rooted in a meaning that can support a brand story without explaining the product literally.

Avoid:

- Obvious AI terms: `ai`, `gpt`, `bot`, `prompt`, `agent`, `neural`, `gen`, `cortex`, `synapse`, `copilot`, `llm`, `smart`.
- Generic SaaS suffixes: `hub`, `ify`, `ly`, `io`, `base`, `stack`, `flow`, `works`, unless the combination is unusually strong.
- Names that over-describe the feature set.
- Forced misspellings that reduce trust.
- Overused mythological or space names unless transformed into something fresh.

## Output Format

For each name, include:

- **Name**: the candidate name.
- **Origin**: source language, domain, object, or concept.
- **Meaning**: plain-English meaning or association.
- **Why it fits**: connect the name to the product's positioning, user, or product promise.
- **Brand perception**: describe how the name is likely to feel in market.
- **Watchouts**: mention pronunciation, spelling, trademark/domain uncertainty, cultural sensitivity, or category confusion when relevant.

Use this compact table for larger lists:

| Name | Origin / Meaning | Why it fits | Brand perception | Watchouts |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Use short sections instead of a table when the user asks for a small, high-consideration shortlist.

## Quality Bar

Do not claim domain, trademark, company-name, or social-handle availability unless you have checked live sources in the current turn. If the user asks for available names, search and verify using current sources before making availability claims.

When a name is inspired by a language or culture, avoid sacred, sensitive, or honorific terms unless the product context justifies them. Prefer neutral concepts such as stone, light, measure, path, vessel, star, bridge, craft, order, signal, boundary, and motion.

End with 2-4 best picks and a concise reason for each when the list has more than eight names.
